If you are reading this, you probably feel like you've hit a wall. You've done the tutorials, you know the basics, but you want to get *actually* good. The secret isn't another 10-hour video course. It's practice.

When it comes to real-world practice, you need a place to test your skills and build your muscle memory. That is where these websites come in, and they are game-changers for any frontend developer wanting to level up.

For daily challenges and a great community, you MUST check out **icodethis.com**. This site gives you new design challenges every single day. They give you the design, and you replicate it with HTML and CSS. It's the best way to get fast and confident with your styling skills.

<WebsiteItemRenderer id="07b27647-6a86-41ea-9a59-9c7f992b7934" client:visible />

But being a great frontend dev isn't just about code, it's about understanding design. **befront.io** is your secret weapon for this. It gives you bite-sized practical tasks and guided tutorials to help you learn design skills. You will learn to think like a designer, which will make your work so much better.

<WebsiteItemRenderer id="5086aa61-ec2b-4f57-bb2d-b794ed080f87" client:visible />

Sometimes you just need to get the fundamentals right. No matter how advanced you are, you will always be looking up HTML tags or attributes. **htmlcheatsheet.com** is a fantastic interactive cheat sheet with code examples and tools. Having this open while you practice is a lifesaver.

<WebsiteItemRenderer id="13b84721-b700-4b42-af97-09eeebd6fb7d" client:visible />

And last but not least, becoming a better developer is also about how you think and solve problems. It is not always about writing code. **Untools.co** is a unique website with a collection of thinking tools and frameworks. It will help you break down problems and make better decisions, which is a true senior developer skill.

<WebsiteItemRenderer id="6f3a5fbb-72f1-4bc3-aa51-6c979b45d8e1" client:visible />

In summary, getting better is not a mystery. It just requires the right kind of practice. Use these sites to challenge yourself, learn new ways of thinking, and your skills will grow faster than you ever thought possible.
